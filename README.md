# HR-Analysis-Dashboard

Overview

This repository contains an Excel dashboard analyzing employee attrition trends at IBM. The dashboard leverages pivot tables and KPIs to uncover relationships between training, income, overtime, satisfaction, and attrition rates. It is designed to provide HR teams and analysts with actionable insights into workforce dynamics.

🔑 Key Features

•	Training vs Attrition
o	Attrition rates vary significantly with training times.

o	Example: Employees with 0 training times show the highest attrition rate (62.5%), while those with 6 training times have much lower attrition (22.2%).

•	Gender-wise Analysis

o	Female employees have slightly higher average income but lower attrition (14.8%) compared to males (17%).

o	Promotion gaps are similar across genders.

•	Income Group Analysis

o	Low-income employees face a much higher attrition rate (28.6%) compared to other groups (11.5%).

o	Highlights the importance of compensation in retention.

•	Overtime & Environment Satisfaction

o	Employees working overtime show higher attrition (27.3%) despite slightly better environment satisfaction scores.

o	Non-overtime employees have much lower attrition (8.5%).

•	KPI Snapshot

o	Average Satisfaction: 2.73

o	Average Work-Life Balance: 2.80

o	Average Involvement: 2.74

o	Total Employees: 546

o	Overall Attrition Rate: 9.7%

📂 Dashboard Contents

•	Pivots: Summarized tables for training, gender, income, and overtime analysis.

•	Data: Raw dataset used for pivot generation.

•	KPI: Key performance indicators for satisfaction, work-life balance, involvement, and attrition.


📈 Insights & Applications

•	Identify high-risk groups (low-income, no training, overtime workers).

•	Support HR in designing training programs and compensation strategies.

•	Improve employee satisfaction and retention by addressing key drivers of attrition.

🛠️ Tech Stack

•	Microsoft Excel (Pivot Tables, Charts, KPIs)

•	Data Visualization & Analysis
